# Privacy Policy — DiskJockey

**Last updated:** 2026-05-29  
**Developer:** Christopher Thomas, Antimatter Studios  
**Contact:** chris.thomas@antimatter-studios.com

---

DiskJockey does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data.

## What we access

DiskJockey accesses files and drives that you explicitly open or connect. This access stays entirely on your device and is never transmitted to any server operated by us.

**Drive and disk image files** — read and written locally when you mount a volume. No content is copied or logged.

**Cloud storage** — when you connect Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, or other providers, authentication is handled directly by macOS and the provider's own app. DiskJockey does not see or store your credentials.

## What we do not do

- We do not collect usage analytics
- We do not collect crash reports
- We do not use advertising frameworks
- We do not share data with third parties
- We do not transmit any data to our servers (we have none)

## macOS sandbox

DiskJockey runs inside the macOS App Sandbox. Its access to your files is limited to what you explicitly grant through the system file picker or by connecting a drive.
